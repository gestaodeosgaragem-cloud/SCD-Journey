What is Sickle Cell Disease?

In Sickle Cell Disease, some blood cells can change shape and move through the bloodstream with more difficulty. This can reduce oxygen transport and, at times, cause blockages in smaller vessels ‚Äî leading to pain and other symptoms.

Trait vs Disease

  • Sickle cell trait: usually doesn‚Äôt cause symptoms like the disease, but it can be passed on.
  • Sickle Cell Disease: happens when a person inherits the genetic change from both parents.

Understanding how your body works is the first step to living better with the condition.

How Sickle Cell Disease can show up

Everyone experiences SCD differently. Some people have few crises; others need more support. What matters most is follow-up and recognizing warning signs.

  • Pain episodes (crises)
  • Fatigue/anemia
  • More frequent infections
  • Jaundice (yellowing of eyes/skin) in some cases
  • Painful swelling in hands/feet in childhood (in some cases)

You don‚Äôt have to ‚Äúpush through.‚Äù Severe pain deserves attention.

When to seek medical care immediately

Some signs require prompt evaluation. Your safety comes first. These are key warning signs to seek care right away:

  • Fever
  • Severe pain outside your usual pattern or that doesn‚Äôt improve
  • Shortness of breath, chest pain, fainting
  • Sudden weakness, neurological changes, confusion
  • Any symptom that scares you

You‚Äôre not overreacting. Seeking help early is care.
